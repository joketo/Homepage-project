Hukkapuku process as a story: The journey of a vintage curtain

The new beginning

The hero of this story is a vintage curtain, whose past is unknown. Maybe it was decorating the window of a small cabin, or maybe it was just waiting its change to shine in a cupboard, forgotten. Nevertheless, somebody had donated it to the fabric reuse center, where it was found in the year 2020. The old curtain was made of synthetic fiber but had so beautiful pattern that it was allowed to join other Hukkapuku fabrics.

Getting clean

After arriving its new home, the curtain went straight to the freezer to wait its turn to be washed. The wash made sure that the curtain was no longer leaking colours or shrinking. Finally, the curtain got to dry outside while enjoying a nice summer breeze.

Choosing the new form

The founder of the curtain inspected the designs of Hukkapuku, inspired by fantasy, nature and history. The model “Uni 2.0” seemed to fit the vintage curtain the best and the transformation began.

New friends

The curtain didn’t want to start its new life all alone, so it was given some new friends: brown linen fabric and cotton ribbon. A nice lining fabric also joined the gang.

Tranformation

The curtain was already starting to forget its old life as a curtain, when entirely new cuts were made to it and neat seams were sewn.

Happy end?

The curtain was starting to be mostly a skirt, but a piece of it was still in the midst of cut waste. The curtain found this sad and decided to do something about it. When it was laid on a fitting doll, it made itself look incomplete. This made the maker grip the last piece of the curtain and add it as a final layer of the skirt. This way, the whole curtain got to be part of the new skirt.
After the final pressing the curtain didn’t feel like a curtain anymore, but a full-blown skirt. And so it started its thrilling wait for its new owner, who would love it as a skirt for the rest of its life.
